Othello

The rules of othello

Othello is a game for two players, played on an 8 by 8 square grid, using pieces which are black on one side and white on the other. The game begins by placing four counters in the centre of the board, two showing black and two showing white.

Black plays first, and the players take turns to place one counter on the board. Each move must trap at least one of your opponent's pieces between the piece you just put down, and one of your pieces already on the board. You can trap pieces in any direction; up, down, sideways or diagonal. The trapped pieces are turned over so they now show your colour.

You must move if you are able to do so, but if you cannot trap any pieces then you lose your turn. If neither player can move, or when the board is full, the game ends. The player with the most pieces of their colour showing on the board at the end of the game is the winner.

Program help

The program can play either side, or both if you're feeling lazy, or neither if you want to play against another person. This is controlled from the Options menu. You can also choose whether to display the legal moves available to each player.

The Game menu starts a new game or quits the program.

The Level menu adjusts the level of computer play; the level number is the number of half-moves the program looks ahead. (A single player's move is a half-move; the opponent's reply makes it a whole move). You can also choose whether to use endgame solving; this allows the computer to look all the way ahead to the end of the game when there are a certain number of moves left, enabling it to play perfectly from that point on.

The Help menu just displays this help file.
